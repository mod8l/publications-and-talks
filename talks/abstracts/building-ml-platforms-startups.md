# Building ML Platforms at Startups: Less Infrastructure, More Outcomes

## Focus

A pragmatic approach to ML platform engineering for fast-moving startups.

## Abstract

Startups do not need a miniature version of Google's ML platform. They need
just enough infrastructure to ship, learn, and iterate without drowning in
undifferentiated work. In this talk I argue for a pragmatic approach to ML
platforms: start with the customer outcome, build the smallest reliable path
to production, and add abstraction only after the same pain appears three
times. I share real trade-offs around feature stores, experiment tracking,
model registries, and monitoring, and explain when to buy, when to build, and
when to defer. The talk is aimed at technical leaders who want to move fast
without painting their teams into a corner. The goal is to help you invest in
the right platform pieces at the right time so your team spends less time
maintaining infrastructure and more time delivering value.

## Takeaways

- Why platform work in startups should be tied to customer outcomes, not
  architectural ideals.
- A simple rule for deciding when to build, buy, or defer ML infrastructure.
- Patterns for feature management, experiment tracking, and monitoring that
  scale from one model to many.
